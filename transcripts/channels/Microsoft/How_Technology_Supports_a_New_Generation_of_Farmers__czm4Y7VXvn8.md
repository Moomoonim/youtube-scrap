## 스크립트

I have 30,000 daughters here. You would not believe me, maybe, but when I walk around the pots, I hear them telling me “We need this.” “We need that.” “Something’s going wrong.” Or “something is going really well.” We learned many aspects of farming from our fathers and grandfathers. We learned when to enter the fields, what should be done, and how to adapt according to the year and conditions. We become attached to our crops almost like they're our children and we enjoy the whole process from planting to harvest. Technology has helped us in many ways, but we can no longer predict the climate. That’s our biggest challenge. Our farmers can monitor their fields using AI powered satellite imagery. Reliability, scalability and sustainable operations are among the most important priorities. That's why our entire infrastructures, runs on Azure cloud. We have 300,000 registered users. For a seven hectare lands like this, you need data. I use AI a lot. I can see how my farm is doing in terms of plant health, in terms of water scarcity, the satellites calculate the amounts of chlorophyl. The biggest contribution of Imece Mobil has been in irrigation. Monitoring the fields— if it would normally take two or three hours, we can reduce that to ten or fifteen minutes. It also predicts weather conditions with about 80–90% accuracy. That's very helpful for us. You know, it's like miracle. So that I can understand which plant has more healthy leaves. This data is the only chance that the plants talk to us. The data is very good, but experience and intuition are important too. We’ve inherited this from our elders, they passed it on to us, and we want to take it further, and pass it on to the next generation.
