## 스크립트

There's a lot of noise in AI right now. Everyone's trying to go fast and break things, and what I'm most proud of is that everyone around me is trying to build things right. I grew up in Brooklyn in a working class neighborhood. Technology was the farthest thing from my mind. I was basically trained to be a musician. I used to read a lot of science magazines. I was so fascinated. Humanist superintelligence to me is about empowering people. It gives you this ability to scale beyond biological limitations. I think humans think the way they speak. If we can build AI that truly understands every voice, regardless of accent, background, literacy... We connect the cool technology with the warm feelings we have and the empathy we have towards people making sure that they're connecting with the things that we build. My parents, for example, they could watch any video that I send them, even though it's like in English or any other languages and it will be transcribed and translated to Vietnamese. The moment I realized this was huge... my 82 year old grandma tried talking to AI for the first time. Copilot knew exactly what she was after. I think the approach that we're taking is very much that of urgency and patience. To me, building responsibly means not taking shortcuts. We really need to slow down and try to take the careful and responsible approach when we deploy these models. I actually tend to think of it as a like a real climb of a mountain. Some of these hard task to solve are going to involve more people than just yourself. I really want to build models and products that I would be proud and happy and comfortable for my kid to use. And feel inspired to get into the world and experience everything around them, and know that technology is helping them do that, not hindering them. The best way to predict the future is to create it. The reason I work on this technology is because I want my kids to live in a better world than we live in here today.
