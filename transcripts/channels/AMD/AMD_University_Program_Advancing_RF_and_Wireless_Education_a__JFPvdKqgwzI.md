## 스크립트

For many years, building radio systems was very expensive and was maybe the privilege of just a few large communications companies. But when it moves to software it becomes more available to more people. Strath SDR stands for Strathclyde Software Defined Radio. So that means that we focus on radios which are flexible and adaptable and we can implement them and design them using programmable chips like the Radio Frequency System on Chip. FPGAs, now in the form of RFSoC, are just essential components of signal processing systems running radar systems, medical imaging systems, mobile wireless. It has to be flexible. If you don't have that capability then you would be building a piece of bespoke hardware for different frequency bands and that's very doable but that's very expensive. We have three courses here where we teach VHDL and FPGAs and how to program and work with those devices. The students acquired so much knowledge they're able to feed that back into the system or even contribute to some of the books that we've written. We've been able to publish four books on AMD technology in the last 15 years. The books themselves are available as PDFs that you can download for free so that anyone can get access. I've had former students who have gone into the working industry who have contacted me afterwards saying, you've got this big fan club down here because of the Zynq book. We love it. It has been a great opportunity and a great privilege to work with the friends of the AMD University program. Without that hardware support, software support and some of the funding support, we would not be where we are today. You have really talented people come in, you put some state-of-the-art equipment on the bench and they just do magic with it. Your teaching on these devices and the RF SoC just helps accelerate an industry because students are coming out prepared, understanding the latest technology, having experienced the latest technology and new generations of RF SoC will go to higher frequency bands. So in some sense, the wireless revolution is just beginning.
