## 스크립트

What if I told you that the leather seats in this AMG aren't really leather at all? They're made from that. Are you sitting comfortably? I'm Rob. I'm your tech explainer, and this is Tech Explained. And this is what those non-leather seats are made of. It's a biotechnological leather alternative. We call it Lab Fiber. So, how do we turn race tires into something that looks, feels, and even smells like real leather? Let's take a look. First, we mechanically recycle tires, not just race tires. We use road tires as well. And that gives us three components. We have this nylon material, the metal within the interior of the tire, and this is what we're after for Lab Fiber, the granules, the rubber itself. It doesn't smell like leather yet. Now, we get to the chemistry. These rubber granules are put through a process called pyrolysis in a vacuum, which doesn't create any CO2. Then, the material that comes out of that, the pyrolysis oil, is spun out to a non-woven fleece material like this. It's lovely and soft. Then, we add this natural protein from agricultural waste to the fleece, and that creates something called dry whites. And it's called dry white because in the leather system, this stage is known as wet white. Those proteins have surrounded every fiber in here, which makes it a lot stiffer now. And then we get to the tanning stage. This is just like natural leather, which after 4 hours of tumbling around turns into this. It smells and feels just like suede. Next, the suede is buffed, so it becomes even softer. And then we apply different finishes to the surface like adding a a grain, so you have different designs and finishes to the finished material. Because Lab Fiber is manufactured, the structure is much stronger than real leather. Let me show you how by introducing the ripper. Just put that in there. First up, let's rip some real leather. This shows how much the leather has stretched, and this is the force applied. So, we can see here that it failed at 250 N. That's really good for leather. And now, the LABELEX fiber. Wow, 800 N. That means LABELEX fiber is more than three times as strong as real leather. And because of all that, we can make the perforations for ventilated seats with bigger holes, allowing more air to get through. That's cool. LABELEX fiber is still in development, and it's coming soon. But, what's already here are the first interiors in the world to be certified by the Vegan Society. You can see them in the all-new electric GLC and all-new electric C-Class. LABELEX fiber seating is just one of the many innovations in tomorrow AM XX.
