## 스크립트

We need to always step back. Take the time to watch and listen. You never know a person's personal situation. Sometimes we need to change our expectations, our ways of working, our level of commitment in order to make change. And so I think in terms of allyship, it's not always easy. The hard decisions and the changes we need to make to get to a better outcome. My brother is gay. But he was in the closet for a very, very long time. I could see that my brother was really hurting without the whole family knowing. He came out maybe ten years ago. And I was so thrilled that earlier this year, I had an opportunity to march in Mardi Gras with Accenture. I think it takes a change in mindset. As I become an ally, how will I behave differently? What will I do differently? What do I have to be more conscious of? What allyship does for us as Innovators is challenge us to change. Usually you’re an ally because you're not in that world. And so as an ally you’re saying “I'm open to learning and I want to show my support.” And I think that is incredibly important. You can only innovate if you are open to change. Accenture is a really powerful organization and we need to think not what have we achieved for Accenture, but what we achieved for the society around us? We have an opportunity to make a big difference.
