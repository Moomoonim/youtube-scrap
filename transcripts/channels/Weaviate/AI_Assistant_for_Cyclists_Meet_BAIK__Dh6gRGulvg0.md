Jinglin, thanks so much for joining us today. It's great to have you here. &gt;&gt; Thank you so much for having me. So, let me tell you the story behind bike. It all started from a very personal experience I had while cycling in San Francisco. I was using my phone to navigate, checking where I could park my bike, looking for the next turn, and I had my eyes glued to the screen while cycling. That's when disaster struck. I got into an accident, fell and injured myself. That moment made me realize there has to be a better way to do this. And I know I'm not alone in this struggle because so many of us who cycle in cities, especially when using bike sharing apps, find ourselves constantly looking at our phones, which is obviously very dangerous. That's how bike was born. B A I K, which also means good in Malay. It's a voice powered cycling assistant that helps you plan and navigate cycling routes based on your preferences, all in real time through voice communication. The whole idea is to keep your eyes off the screen and on the road where they belong. What makes bike special is that it has a very clean interface because we don't want our eyes constantly on the screen. Instead, we communicate with it entirely through voice. But behind the scenes, even though the user just has to press one button, there's actually a sophisticated crew of AI agents built using Crew AI that's working together on different tasks. Let me break down the architecture for you. First, there's the voice agent that gathers user requests. This gets passed to another agent that identifies the start and end destinations and begin searching for directions. Then there's another agent equipped with tools that can semantically understand user preferences. So, if someone says anything like avoiding hills or finding a flat route, the agent identifies and store those preferences in a wevate vector database. There's also an agent dedicated to finding the nearest bike sharing stations. And finally, there's an orchestrating agent that puts everything together and passes it back to the voice agent to communicate with the user. &gt;&gt; Hello, Baker here.
